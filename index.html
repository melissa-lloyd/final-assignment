What was the Spanish Empire?

The Spanish Empire were the territories under the Spanish monarchy from the late 15th century up until the 19th century. It became a global power, dominating the oceans and colonies in America, Europe, Africa, Asia and Oceania. Spain enjoyed a cultural golden age in the sixteenth and seventeenth centuries when silver and gold from American mines increasingly financed a long series of European and North African wars. Until the eighteenth century, the Spanish empire was the largest in the world, although it suffered fluctuating military and economic fortunes. Numerous conflicts and a weak central governemnt lead Spain to lose its colonies and worldly influence.

What were the goals for colonization?

  1. Increase trade for Spain
    Even though their new territory was not Asia like they thought it was, it still allowed them to create new trade routes and trade entirely new products.
  2. Spread religion
    As Catholics, Spanish colonists felt they had to "save" the natives by spreading their religion, preventing the Protestants from doing the same at all costs
  3. Exploit the resources of the newly-found land
    They were looking for precious metals, gold and silver, and to their fortune, they found extremely rich deposits in their colonies.
