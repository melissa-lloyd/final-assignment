<h2>The Viceroyalty of New Spain</h2>
<p>The territory south of Canada and north of Panama, including all the islands of the Caribbean, was all part of the viceroyalty of New Spain. 
The viceroyalty of New Spain included USA, Mexico, Cuba, Dominican Republic, Puerto Rico, Haiti, Jamaica, Trinidad and Tobago, Belize, Guatemala, Honduras, El Salvador, Nicaragua, and Costa Rica. 
However, the viceroyalty was divided into smaller divisions for administrative purposes. These were:</p>
<ul>
	<li>New Spain</li>
	<li>Captaincy General of Cuba</li>
	<li>Captaincy General of Guatemala</li>
</ul>

<br> <br>

<h2>New Spain</h2>
<img class="nueva-espana" src="https://mxcity.mx/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/mxcity.mx_imagen-portada-2-300x150.jpg" alt="Life in Mexico City during the colonial period">
<p>New Spain was made up of:</p>
<ul>
	<li>Parts of USA</li>
	<li>Mexico</li>
</ul>
<p>It`s year of acquisition was 1521, with the fall of the Aztecs. It`s year of divestiture was 1821, through the Mexican War of Independence.</p>

<br>

<h2>Captaincy General of Cuba</h2>
<img class="cap-cuba" src="https://www.curiosfera.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/%C3%A9poca-colonial-de-Cuba.jpg" alt="Life in Cuba during the colonial period">
<p>The Captaincy General of Cuba was made up of:</p>
<ul>
	<li>Cuba</li>
	<li>Dominican Republic</li>
	<li>Puerto Rico</li>
	<li>Jamaica</li>
	<li>Trinidad and Tobago</li>
	<li>Florida</li>
</ul>
<p>It`s year of acquisition was 1511, but became its own captaincy in 1607. It`s year of divestiture was between 1670-1898 as different Caribbean islands gained independence in separate armed conflicts or were handed over to other empires.</p>

<br>

<h2>Captaincy General of Guatemala</h2>
<img class="cap-guatemala" src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/52/Companiadejesus.jpg" alt="Colonial Guatemala">
<p>The Captaincy General of Guatemala was made up of:</p>
<ul>
	<li>Belize</li>
	<li>Guatemala</li>
	<li>El Salvador</li>
	<li>Nicaragua</li>
	<li>Costa Rica</li>
</ul>
<p>It`s year of acquisition was around 1540, when the city of Guatemala was established. It`s year of divestiture was 1821, through the Mexican War of Independence.</p>

<h2 id="today">How are the colonies doing today?</h2>

<div id="today">
<h3>Mexico</h3>
<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexico" target="_blank">
	<img class="mexico-pic" src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Flag_of_Mexico.svg/2000px-Flag_of_Mexico.svg.png" alt="Mexican flag">
</a>
</div>
<ul>
	<li><strong>Government after divestiture:</strong> Short-lived monarchy. 3 years later, Mexico established a federal republic (democratic)</li>
	<li><strong>Episodes of turmoil after independence:</strong> Spanish attempt to recolonize, military interventions from the Americans and French, Second Mexican Empire, 30-year long dictatorship, 11-year long civil war</li>
	<li><strong>Government now:</strong> Democratic - federal republic. Left-wing party in power. Current president: Andres Manuel Lopez Obrador</li>
	<li><strong>Situation today:</strong> Politically stable, GDP of 1.15 trillion USD and growing at a 1.3% rate, ranks 138 out of 175 in least corrupt countries list, insecurity on the rise, war on drugs violent and mostly ineffective</li>
</ul>

<div id="today">
<h3>Cuba</h3>
<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cuba" target="_blank">
	<img class="cuba-pic" src="https://www.worldatlas.com/r/w728-h425-c728x425/upload/68/b6/f5/untitled-design-323.jpg" alt="Cuban flag">
</a>
</div>
<ul>
	<li><strong>Government after divestiture:</strong> Semi-independent republic with heavy US intervention</li>
	<li><strong>Episodes of turmoil after independence:</strong> Dictatorial regimes from Manchado and Batista and the coups that were required to overthrow them, guerrillas with Fidel Castro and Che Guevara, stuck between the US and USSR in the Cold War, US economic embargo of the island</li>
	<li><strong>Government now:</strong> One-party, socialist, communist republic. Current president: Miguel Diaz-Canel</li>
	<li><strong>Situation today:</strong> Politically stable, still sanctioned by the US, GDP of 87.13 billion USD and growing at a 1.8% rate, ranks 65 of 175 in least corrupt countries list</li>
</ul>
