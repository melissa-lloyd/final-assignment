The Viceroyalty of Peru

South America was colonized by the Spanish and the Portuguese. The Spanish colonies in this territory were part or the viceroyalty of Peru. It included the modern day countries of Panama, Colombia, Venezuela, Ecuador, Peru, Bolivia, Uruguay, Paraguay, Argentina, and Chile. The viceroyalty was then divided into smaller divisions for administrative purposes. These were:

  • New Granada
  • Peru
  • Rio de la Plata
  • Captaincy General of Chile


New Granada

New Granada was made up of:

  • Panama
  • Colombia
  • Venezuela
  • Ecuador
  • Parts of Guyana

It`s year of acquisition was 1550, when it became the new colony of Granada, although it was annexed to the Viceroyalty of Peru. It`s year of divestiture was 1819, after the independence movement led by Simon Bolivar created the country of Gran Colombia.


Peru


Peru was made up of:

  • Peru
  • Parts of Bolivia

It`s year of acquisition was 1542, with the fall of the Incas. It`s year of divestiture was 1824, through the Peruvian War of Independence.


Rio de la Plata

Rio de la Plata was made up of:

  • Bolivia
  • Uruguay
  • Paraguay
  • Argentina

It`s year of acquisition was around 1580 with the second establishment of the city of Buenos Aires. It was part of viceroyalty of Peru, but it finally became its viceroyalty (viceroyalty of Rio de la Plata) in 1776. It`s year of divestiture was 1816, through the Argentine War of Independence.


Captaincy General of Chile

The Captaincy General of Chile was made up of:

  • Chile

It`s year of acquisition was 1541, when the city of Santiago was founded. It`s year of divestiture was 1818, through the Chilean War of Independence.



How are the colonies doing today?

Peru

  • Government after divestiture: Arbitrary rules of military caudillos
  • Episodes of turmoil after independence: At least 24 regime changes between democratic and military governments, Constitution rewritten 6 times, War of the Pacific with Chile and Bolivia, economic inflation of the 1980s and terrorist groups
  • Government now: Democratic republic with a semi-presidential system. Current president: Martin Vizcarra. Current prime-minister: Salvador del Solar
  • Situation today: Politically stable, GDP of 211.4 billion USD growing at a 2.5% rate, ranks 105 of 175 in least corrupt countries list, involved in Odebrecht scandal
